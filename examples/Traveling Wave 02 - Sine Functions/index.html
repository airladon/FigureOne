Sine Waves

In a right angle triangle, the sine function relates angle, hypotenuase and opposite side. \[\sin\theta={opposite \over hypotenuse}\]

When the angle \(\theta\) is plotted on a Cartesian axis, the sine function “looks like” a wave.

When \(a \ne 0\), there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are \[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.\]
